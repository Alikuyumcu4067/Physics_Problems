# Technical Analysis: Proton Levitation in a Uniform Electric Field
**Subject:** Electromagnetism I | **Task:** 05

---

### Phase 1: Problem Identification
The objective is to calculate the specific **Electric Field Intensity ($\vec{E}$)** required to maintain a proton in a state of static equilibrium near the Earth's surface. In this state, the particle remains stationary, effectively "levitating."

---

### Phase 2: Force Identification and Free Body Diagram
To achieve levitation, we must analyze the two primary forces acting on the proton:

1.  **Gravitational Force ($\vec{F}_g$):** Acts vertically downward toward the center of the Earth.
    * Formula: $F_g = m_p \cdot g$
2.  **Electric Force ($\vec{F}_e$):** Must act vertically upward to oppose gravity.
    * Formula: $F_e = q \cdot E$



---

### Phase 3: Mathematical Derivation
For the proton to remain at rest, the **Net Force ($\sum F$)** must be zero according to Newton's First Law:

$$\sum F_y = 0 \implies F_e - F_g = 0$$

Equating the two forces:
$$q \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$

Rearranging the equation to solve for the magnitude of the Electric Field ($E$):
$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{q}$$

---

### Phase 4: Numerical Substitution
We utilize the standard physical constants for a proton:
* **Proton Mass ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Elementary Charge ($q$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Gravity ($g$):** $9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$

Substituting these into our derived formula:
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \cdot (9.81)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}$$

---

### Phase 5: Quantitative Result
Performing the calculation:
$$E \approx 1.024 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$$

**Final Value:** The required electric field strength is approximately **$1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ Volts per meter}$**.

---

### Phase 6: Analytical Conclusion
* **Magnitude Observation:** The required field is incredibly small (on the order of $10^{-7}$). 
* **Physical Interpretation:** This result highlights the massive disparity between the **Stronger Electromagnetic Force** and the **Weaker Gravitational Force**. Even an extremely weak electric field is sufficient to overcome the gravitational pull of an entire planet on a subatomic particle.
