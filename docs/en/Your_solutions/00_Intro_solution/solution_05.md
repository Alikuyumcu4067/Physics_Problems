## 5. Trigonometry in Physics

**Problem:**
A force of $F = 100 \text{ N}$ is applied at an angle of $\theta = 30^\circ$ to the horizontal. Find the horizontal ($F_x$) and vertical ($F_y$) components of this force.

**Key Formulas:**
* Horizontal component: $F_x = F \cdot \cos(\theta)$
* Vertical component: $F_y = F \cdot \sin(\theta)$

**Given Values:**
* $F = 100 \text{ N}$
* $\theta = 30^\circ$
* $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$
* $\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Calculate $F_x$:**
   $F_x = 100 \cdot \cos(30^\circ)$
   $F_x = 100 \cdot 0.866$
   $\mathbf{F_x = 86.6 \text{ N}}$

2. **Calculate $F_y$:**
   $F_y = 100 \cdot \sin(30^\circ)$
   $F_y = 100 \cdot 0.5$
   $\mathbf{F_y = 50 \text{ N}}$

**Final Result:**
The force components are $F_x = 86.6 \text{ N}$ and $F_y = 50 \text{ N}$.
