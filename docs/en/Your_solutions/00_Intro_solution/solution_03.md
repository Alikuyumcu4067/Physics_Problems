## 3. Proportionality in Physics
**Problem:** According to Newton's Law of Gravitation: $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
What happens to the force $F$ if the distance $r$ doubles and both masses ($m_1, m_2$) are halved?

**Solution Steps:**
1. **Distance ($r$) changes:** If $r \to 2r$, then the denominator becomes $(2r)^2 = 4r^2$. This means the force decreases by **4 times** because of the inverse square law.
2. **Masses ($m$) change:** If both $m_1 \to m_1/2$ and $m_2 \to m_2/2$, the product $m_1 \cdot m_2$ becomes $(1/2) \cdot (1/2) = 1/4$. This means the force decreases by another **4 times**.

**Total Calculation:**
$$F_{new} = G \frac{(m_1/2) (m_2/2)}{(2r)^2} = G \frac{1/4 \cdot m_1 m_2}{4 r^2} = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$

**Result:**
The new force is **1/16** of the original force. The force becomes 16 times smaller.
