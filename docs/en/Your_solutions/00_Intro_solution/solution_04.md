## 4. Rearranging Formulas - The Pendulum Formula

**Problem:**
Rearrange the pendulum period formula to solve for the acceleration due to gravity ($g$):
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

**Step-by-Step Derivation:**

1. **Original formula:**
   $$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

2. **Isolate the square root:**
   Divide both sides by $2\pi$:
   $$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

3. **Remove the square root:**
   Square both sides of the equation:
   $$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$

4. **Expand the square:**
   $$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

5. **Isolate $g$:**
   Multiply both sides by $g$ and then rearrange to solve for $g$:
   $$g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L$$
   $$\mathbf{g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}$$

**Final Result:**
The rearranged formula for gravity is:  
$$\mathbf{g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}$$
