## 9. Solving Quadratic Equations

**Problem:**
Solve the quadratic equation $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$ for $x$.

**Key Formula:**
For $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the roots are:
$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

1. **Identify the coefficients:**
   * $a = 1$
   * $b = -5$
   * $c = 6$

2. **Calculate the Discriminant ($\Delta$):**
   $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac = (-5)^2 - 4(1)(6)$
   $\Delta = 25 - 24 = 1$

3. **Apply the Quadratic Formula:**
   $x = \frac{-(-5) \pm \sqrt{1}}{2(1)}$
   $x = \frac{5 \pm 1}{2}$

4. **Find the two roots:**
   * $x_1 = \frac{5 + 1}{2} = 3$
   * $x_2 = \frac{5 - 1}{2} = 2$

**Final Result:**
The solutions (roots) are **x = 3** and **x = 2**.
