## 6. Unit Conversion

**Problem:**
A car is traveling at a constant speed of $v = 72 \text{ km/h}$. Convert this speed to meters per second ($\text{m/s}$).

**Step-by-Step Conversion:**

1. **Conversion Factors:**
   * $1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m}$
   * $1 \text{ h} = 3600 \text{ s}$ ($60 \text{ min} \times 60 \text{ s}$)

2. **Calculation:**
   $$v = \frac{72 \text{ km}}{1 \text{ h}} \times \left( \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ km}} \right) \times \left( \frac{1 \text{ h}}{3600 \text{ s}} \right)$$

3. **Simplify the Numbers:**
   $$v = \frac{72 \times 1000}{3600} \text{ m/s}$$
   $$v = \frac{72000}{3600} \text{ m/s}$$
   $$\mathbf{v = 20 \text{ m/s}}$$

**Final Result:**
The speed of the car is **20 m/s**.
