## 6. Solar Gravity (Technical Analysis)

### Step 1: Quantify Given Parameters (SI Units)
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.6743 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3 \text{kg}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2}$
* **Sun's Mass ($M_S$):** $2.0 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}$
* **Sun's Radius ($R_S$):** $6.96 \times 10^{8} \text{ m}$
* **Standard Earth Gravity ($g_{\text{Earth}}$):** $9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$

### Step 2: Calculate Surface Gravity ($g_S$)
Applying Newton's surface gravity formulation:
$$g_S = \frac{GM_S}{R_S^2}$$

Substituting the scientific constants:
$$g_S = \frac{(6.6743 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}) \times (2.0 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg})}{(6.96 \times 10^{8} \text{ m})^2}$$

$$g_S = \frac{1.33486 \times 10^{20}}{4.84416 \times 10^{17}} \approx 275.56 \text{ m/s}^2$$

### Step 3: Determine Weight Proportionality Factor ($\kappa$)
The weight scaling factor is determined by the direct ratio of the two local gravitational fields:
$$\kappa = \frac{g_S}{g_{\text{Earth}}}$$

$$\kappa = \frac{275.56 \text{ m/s}^2}{9.81 \text{ m/s}^2} \approx 28.09$$

---

> **Conclusion:** The calculated acceleration due to gravity on the solar surface is **$275.56 \text{ m/s}^2$**, yielding a weight inflation factor of **$28.09$**.
