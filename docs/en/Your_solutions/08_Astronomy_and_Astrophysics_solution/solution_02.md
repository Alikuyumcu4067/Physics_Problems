## 2. Orbital Mechanics (Step-by-Step)

### Part 1: Orbital Speed of the ISS ($v_{\text{ISS}}$)

#### Step 1: Calculate Total Orbital Radius ($r$)
The orbital radius must be measured from the center of the Earth.
$$r = R_E + \text{altitude}$$
$$r = 6378 \text{ km} + 400 \text{ km} = 6778 \text{ km} = 6.778 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$$

#### Step 2: Apply the Orbital Velocity Formula
Using $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$ and $M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$:
$$v_{\text{ISS}} = \sqrt{\frac{G M_E}{r}}$$
$$v_{\text{ISS}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{6.778 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_{\text{ISS}} = \sqrt{\frac{3.9844 \times 10^{14}}{6.778 \times 10^6}} \approx 7,667 \text{ m/s} \approx 7.67 \text{ km/s}$$

---

### Part 2: Orbital Speed of Earth Around the Sun ($v_{\text{Earth}}$)

#### Step 1: Convert Time Period ($T$) to Seconds
$$T = 365.25 \text{ days} \times 24 \text{ h/day} \times 3600 \text{ s/h} \approx 3.1558 \times 10^7 \text{ s}$$

#### Step 2: Calculate Speed Using Circumference
Using the Earth-Sun distance $R = 150 \times 10^6 \text{ km} = 1.5 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$:
$$v_{\text{Earth}} = \frac{2\pi R}{T}$$
$$v_{\text{Earth}} = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 1.5 \times 10^{11}}{3.1558 \times 10^7} \approx 29,865 \text{ m/s} \approx 29.86 \text{ km/s}$$

---

### Part 3: Comparison

* **ISS Velocity:** $\approx 7.67 \text{ km/s}$  
* **Earth Velocity:** $\approx 29.86 \text{ km/s}$  

> **Result:** **Earth's orbital speed around the Sun** is faster than the ISS around the Earth by a factor of roughly 3.9.
