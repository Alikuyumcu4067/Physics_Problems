## 8. Interplanetary Travel (Kinematic Analysis)

### Step 1: System Baseline
* **Target Linear Distance ($x$):** $55 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$
* **Governing Equation:** $t = \frac{x}{v}$

### Step 2: Part (a) - Electromagnetic/Light Signal
* **Velocity ($c$):** $300,000 \text{ km/s}$
$$t_a = \frac{55 \times 10^6 \text{ km}}{300,000 \text{ km/s}} \approx 183.33 \text{ s}$$
$$\text{Converted Time:} \quad t_a = \frac{183.33}{60} \approx 3.06 \text{ minutes}$$

### Step 3: Part (b) - Deep Space Probe Flight Path
* **Velocity ($v$):** $40,000 \text{ km/h}$
$$t_b = \frac{55 \times 10^6 \text{ km}}{40,000 \text{ km/h}} = 1,375 \text{ hours}$$
$$\text{Converted Time:} \quad t_b = \frac{1,375}{24} \approx 57.29 \text{ days}$$

### Step 4: Part (c) - Hypothetical Jet Atmospheric Flight
* **Velocity ($v$):** $900 \text{ km/h}$
$$t_c = \frac{55 \times 10^6 \text{ km}}{900 \text{ km/h}} \approx 61,111.11 \text{ hours}$$
$$\text{Converted Time (Days):} \quad \frac{61,111.11}{24} \approx 2,546.3 \text{ days}$$
$$\text{Converted Time (Years):} \quad \frac{2,546.3}{365.25} \approx 6.97 \text{ years}$$

---

> **Conclusion:** Calculated transport durations are exactly **$3.06 \text{ minutes}$** for electromagnetic communication, **$57.29 \text{ days}$** for ballistic spacecraft, and **$6.97 \text{ years}$** via conventional commercial aviation.
