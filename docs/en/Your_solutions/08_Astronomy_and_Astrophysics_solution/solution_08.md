### 8. Interplanetary Travel (Step-by-Step Calculation)

**Given Data:**
* Distance ($d$) = $55\text{ million km} = 55,000,000\text{ km} = 55 \times 10^6\text{ km}$
* Basic Motion Formula: 
    $$\text{Time } (t) = \frac{\text{Distance } (d)}{\text{Speed } (v)}$$

---

#### Part a) A Message Sent at the Speed of Light
The speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) is approximately $300,000\text{ km/s}$ ($3 \times 10^5\text{ km/s}$).

* **Step 1: Apply the formula**
    $$t_a = \frac{55,000,000\text{ km}}{300,000\text{ km/s}}$$
    $$t_a \approx 183.33\text{ seconds}$$

* **Step 2: Convert seconds to minutes**
    $$t_a = \frac{183.33\text{ seconds}}{60\text{ seconds/minute}} \approx \mathbf{3.06\text{ minutes}}$$

* **Conclusion:** It takes **3 minutes and 4 seconds** for a signal to travel one way between the two planets at their closest approach.

---

#### Part b) A Spacecraft Traveling at a Constant Speed of $40,000\text{ km/h}$
The constant velocity of the spacecraft ($v_b$) is $40,000\text{ km/h}$.

* **Step 1: Apply the formula to find total hours**
    $$t_b = \frac{55,000,000\text{ km}}{40,000\text{ km/h}}$$
    $$t_b = 1,375\text{ hours}$$

* **Step 2: Convert hours to days**
    $$t_b = \frac{1,375\text{ hours}}{24\text{ hours/day}} \approx \mathbf{57.29\text{ days}}$$

* **Conclusion:** Moving in a theoretical perfect straight line, the spacecraft would complete the journey in **57 days and 7 hours**.

---

#### Part c) An "Airplane" Traveling at a Constant Speed of $900\text{ km/h}$
The constant velocity of a typical commercial jet ($v_c$) is $900\text{ km/h}$.

* **Step 1: Apply the formula to find total hours**
    $$t_c = \frac{55,000,000\text{ km}}{900\text{ km/h}}$$
    $$t_c \approx 61,111.11\text{ hours}$$

* **Step 2: Convert hours to days**
    $$\text{Days} = \frac{61,111.11\text{ hours}}{24\text{ hours/day}} \approx 2,546.30\text{ days}$$

* **Step 3: Convert days to Earth years** (Using a standard calendar year of $365.25$ days)
    $$t_c = \frac{2,546.30\text{ days}}{365.25\text{ days/year}} \approx \mathbf{6.97\text{ years}}$$

* **Conclusion:** A commercial airliner flying continuously through space would take nearly **7 years** to arrive at Mars.
