# 1. Rotational Velocity of Earth

To calculate the linear speed of a point on Earth’s equator due to Earth’s rotation, we use the formula:

\[
v = \frac{2\pi R}{T}
\]

where:

- \(R = 6378\ \text{km}\) → Earth's radius
- \(T = 24\ \text{hours} = 86400\ \text{s}\) → Earth's rotation period

---

## Step 1 — Calculate Earth's Circumference

\[
2\pi R = 2\pi(6378)
\]

\[
2\pi R \approx 40074\ \text{km}
\]

So, the circumference of Earth at the equator is approximately:

\[
40074\ \text{km}
\]

---

## Step 2 — Divide by the Rotation Period

\[
v = \frac{40074}{86400}
\]

\[
v \approx 0.465\ \text{km/s}
\]

---

## Final Answer

\[
\boxed{v \approx 0.465\ \text{km/s}}
\]

Therefore, a point on Earth’s equator moves at approximately:

- \(0.465\ \text{km/s}\)
- \(465\ \text{m/s}\)
- \(1670\ \text{km/h}\)
