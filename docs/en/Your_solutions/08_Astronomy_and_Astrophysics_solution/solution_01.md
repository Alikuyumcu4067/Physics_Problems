## Problem 1 — Rotational Velocity of Earth's Equator

**Given:** Earth's radius $R_E \approx 6378\ \text{km}$, rotation period $T = 24\ \text{h} = 86400\ \text{s}$

The linear (tangential) speed of a point on the equator is:

$$v = \frac{2\pi R_E}{T}$$

$$v = \frac{2\pi \times 6378\ \text{km}}{86400\ \text{s}} = \frac{40074\ \text{km}}{86400\ \text{s}}$$

$$\boxed{v \approx 0.4638\ \text{km/s} \approx 463.8\ \text{m/s}}$$
