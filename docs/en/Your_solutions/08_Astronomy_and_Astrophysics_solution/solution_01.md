## 🌍 Rotational Velocity of Earth's Equator

**Given:**
- Point on Earth's equator
- Earth's radius: $R_E \approx 6378\ \text{km}$
- Rotation period: $T = 24\ \text{h} = 86400\ \text{s}$

---

### 🎯 Objective

Find the linear speed of a point on the Earth's equator due to its rotation.

---

### 📐 Step 1: Use the Linear Speed Formula

$$v = \frac{2\pi R_E}{T}$$

> - A point on the equator travels the full circumference in one day
> - Linear speed = distance / time

---

### 📌 Step 2: Calculate the Circumference

$$2\pi R_E = 2\pi \times 6378\ \text{km} \approx 40074\ \text{km}$$

---

### 📌 Step 3: Substitute into the Formula

$$v = \frac{40074\ \text{km}}{86400\ \text{s}}$$

---

### 📌 Step 4: Perform the Calculation

$$\boxed{v \approx 0.4638\ \text{km/s} \approx 463.8\ \text{m/s}}$$

---

### ✅ Physical Interpretation

- A point on the equator moves at nearly **1670 km/h**
- This is about **1.4× the speed of sound** (~340 m/s)
- We don't feel this motion because everything on Earth rotates together
