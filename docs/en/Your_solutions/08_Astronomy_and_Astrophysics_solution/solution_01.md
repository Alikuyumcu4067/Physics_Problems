## 1. Rotational Velocity

To find the linear speed ($v$) of a point on the equator, we divide the total distance traveled in one rotation (the Earth's equatorial circumference) by the time it takes to complete that rotation.

### Given Parameters:
* **Earth's Radius ($R_E$):** $\approx 6378 \text{ km}$
* **Rotation Period ($T$):** 24 hours (one solar day)
    * Converting to seconds: $24 \times 60 \times 60 = 86,400 \text{ s}$

### Formula & Calculation:
The linear velocity formula is:
$$v = \frac{2\pi R_E}{T}$$

Substituting the values into the equation:
$$v = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 6378 \text{ km}}{86,400 \text{ s}}$$

$$v \approx \frac{40,074.15 \text{ km}}{86,400 \text{ s}}$$

$$v \approx 0.4638 \text{ km/s}$$

---

> **Result:** The linear speed of a point on the Earth's equator due to its rotation is approximately **$0.464 \text{ km/s}$** (which is roughly $1,670 \text{ km/h}$).
