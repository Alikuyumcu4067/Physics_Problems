## 3. Microgravity (Step-by-Step)

### Part 1: Calculation of Gravity at ISS Altitude

#### Step 1: Define Constants and Variables
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
* **Mass of Earth ($M_E$):** $5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
* **Total Orbital Radius ($r$):** $R_E + h = 6378 \text{ km} + 400 \text{ km} = 6778 \text{ km} = 6.778 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

#### Step 2: Use Newton's Gravitational Acceleration Formula
$$g_{\text{ISS}} = \frac{G M_E}{r^2}$$

#### Step 3: Compute the Value
$$g_{\text{ISS}} = \frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{(6.778 \times 10^6)^2}$$
$$g_{\text{ISS}} = \frac{3.9844 \times 10^{14}}{4.5941 \times 10^{13}} \approx 8.67 \text{ m/s}^2$$

---

### Part 2: Why Astronauts Experience "Weightlessness"

#### The Mechanism of Free Fall:
1. Earth's gravity at $400 \text{ km}$ is still highly active, operating at roughly **88%** of surface gravity ($8.67 \text{ m/s}^2$).
2. The ISS travels horizontally at a rapid speed ($\approx 7.67 \text{ km/s}$). As gravity pulls it down, the Earth's surface curves away at the exact same rate.
3. Therefore, the ISS and everything inside it are **constantly falling around the Earth**.
4. Because the astronauts and the space station fall together, there is no scale or floor pushing up against them to create a sensation of weight (absence of normal force).

---

> **Result:** The acceleration due to gravity at the ISS is **$8.67 \text{ m/s}^2$**. Astronauts float purely because they are in a state of continuous **free fall**, not because gravity is zero.
