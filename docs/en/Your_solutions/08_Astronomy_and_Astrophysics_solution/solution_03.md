### 3. Microgravity at ISS Altitude (Step-by-Step Calculation)

**Objective:** Find the gravitational acceleration at ISS altitude and explain why astronauts feel weightless.

---

#### **Given Data:**
* ISS altitude ($h$) = $400\text{ km}$
* Earth's radius ($R_E$) = $6378\text{ km}$
* Surface gravity ($g_0$) = $9.81\text{ m/s}^2$

---

#### **Step-by-Step Solution:**

* **Step 1: Use the Gravitational Acceleration Formula** Gravity decreases with the square of the distance from Earth's center, following the proportional formula:
  
  $$g_h = g_0 \left( \frac{R_E}{R_E + h} \right)^2$$

* **Step 2: Calculate the Ratio** First, find the ratio of Earth's radius to the total distance from the Earth's center to the ISS:
  
  $$\frac{R_E}{R_E + h} = \frac{6378}{6378 + 400} = \frac{6378}{6778} \approx 0.9410$$

* **Step 3: Substitute into the Formula** Square the ratio and multiply it by the surface gravity ($g_0$):
  
  $$g_h = 9.81 \times (0.9410)^2 = 9.81 \times 0.8855$$
  
  $$g_h \approx \mathbf{8.68\text{ m/s}^2}$$
  
  *This is about **88.5%** of surface gravity — far from zero!*

* **Step 4: Why Do Astronauts Feel Weightless?** Despite $g_h \approx 8.68\text{ m/s}^2$, astronauts experience weightlessness because:
  * The ISS and everything inside it are in **continuous free fall** toward Earth.
  * The ISS moves forward fast enough to **keep missing** Earth as it falls.
  * Both the astronaut and the spacecraft accelerate at **exactly the same rate**.
  * There is **no normal (contact) force** between them $\rightarrow$ causing the sensation of weightlessness.

$$\text{Weightlessness} \neq \text{Zero gravity} \quad \text{(it is orbital free fall)}$$

---

#### **Physical Interpretation:**

| Location | Gravitational Acceleration |
| :--- | :--- |
| Earth's surface | $9.81\text{ m/s}^2$ |
| ISS altitude (400 km) | $8.68\text{ m/s}^2$ |
| **Reduction** | **$\approx 11.5\%$ less** |

* This phenomenon is called **microgravity**, not zero gravity.
* An astronaut with a mass of $70\text{ kg}$ would still experience a gravitational force of **$\approx 608\text{ N}$** at ISS altitude.
