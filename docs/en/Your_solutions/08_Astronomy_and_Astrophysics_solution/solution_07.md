## 7. Megastructures: Dyson Sphere (Technical Analysis)

### Step 1: Establish System Constraints
* **Mass Budget ($M$):** $3.3 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$ (Total mass of Mercury)
* **Surface Mass Density ($\sigma$):** $10 \text{ kg/m}^2$

### Step 2: Compute Maximum Constructible Surface Area ($A$)
The surface area is dictated by dividing the total material mass by the material density per square meter:
$$A = \frac{M}{\sigma}$$
$$A = \frac{3.3 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}}{10 \text{ kg/m}^2} = 3.3 \times 10^{22} \text{ m}^2$$

### Step 3: Isolate and Calculate the Megastructure Radius ($r$)
Using the standard geometric surface area formula for a sphere ($A = 4\pi r^2$):
$$r = \sqrt{\frac{A}{4\pi}}$$

Substituting the computed area:
$$r = \sqrt{\frac{3.3 \times 10^{22} \text{ m}^2}{4\pi}}$$
$$r = \sqrt{2.62605 \times 10^{21} \text{ m}^2} \approx 5.1245 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}$$

Converting the metric output to kilometers:
$$r \approx 5.12 \times 10^7 \text{ km} = 51.25 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$$

---

> **Conclusion:** The resulting radius of the hollow solar-panel megastructure would be exactly **$5.12 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}$** (or **$51.25 \text{ million km}$**).
