### 7. Megastructures (Dyson Sphere - Step-by-Step Calculation)

A "Dyson Sphere" is a hypothetical megastructure that completely encompasses a star to capture its total energy output. In this problem, we calculate the radius of such a sphere if the entire mass of the planet Mercury is converted into solar panels.

---

#### **Given Data:**
* Mass of Mercury ($M$) = $3.3 \times 10^{23}\text{ kg}$
* Surface density of the solar panels ($\sigma$) = $10\text{ kg/m}^2$
* Surface area of a sphere formula: $A = 4\pi R^2$

---

#### **Step-by-Step Solution:**

* **Step 1: Calculate the total surface area ($A$) of the solar panel sphere**
  
  The total area that can be produced is equal to the total available mass divided by the required mass per square meter (surface density):
  
  $$A = \frac{M}{\sigma}$$
  
  $$A = \frac{3.3 \times 10^{23}\text{ kg}}{10\text{ kg/m}^2} = 3.3 \times 10^{22}\text{ m}^2$$

* **Step 2: Set up the equation for the radius ($R$)**
  
  Using the surface area formula of a sphere ($A = 4\pi R^2$), we isolate the radius ($R$):
  
  $$4\pi R^2 = 3.3 \times 10^{22}\text{ m}^2$$
  
  $$R^2 = \frac{3.3 \times 10^{22}}{4\pi}$$
  
  $$R = \sqrt{\frac{3.3 \times 10^{22}}{4\pi}}$$

* **Step 3: Calculate the radius in meters**
  
  $$R = \sqrt{\frac{3.3 \times 10^{22}}{12.56637}} \approx \sqrt{2.62526 \times 10^{21}}$$
  
  $$R \approx 5.1237 \times 10^{10}\text{ meters}$$

* **Step 4: Convert the radius into standard astronomical units**

  * **In Kilometers:**
    $$R \approx \mathbf{51,237,000\text{ km}}$$
    
  * **In Astronomical Units (AU):** (Where $1\text{ AU} \approx 149.6 \times 10^6\text{ km}$)
    $$\text{Radius in AU} = \frac{51.237 \times 10^6\text{ km}}{149.6 \times 10^6\text{ km/AU}} \approx \mathbf{0.342\text{ AU}}$$

---

#### **Conclusion & Context:**

The calculated radius of the Dyson Sphere is approximately **$0.34\text{ AU}$**. 

To put this into perspective, the actual average orbital distance of Mercury from the Sun is about **$0.39\text{ AU}$**. This means that the material from Mercury is mathematically sufficient to build a complete energy-harvesting shell almost exactly at its own current orbit around the Sun.
