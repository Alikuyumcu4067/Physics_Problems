## 5. Escape Velocity (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Identify and Convert Given Values
* **Moon's Mass ($M_M$):** $7.35 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$
* **Moon's Radius ($R_M$):** $1737 \text{ km} = 1.737 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
* **Earth's Escape Velocity:** $\approx 11.2 \text{ km/s}$

### Step 2: Apply the Escape Velocity Formula
$$v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM_M}{R_M}}$$

Substituting the values:
$$v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 7.35 \times 10^{22}}{1.737 \times 10^6}}$$

### Step 3: Compute the Numerical Value
$$v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{9.81078 \times 10^{12}}{1.737 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{5,648,117.44} \approx 2,376.6 \text{ m/s}$$

Converting meters per second to kilometers per second:
$$v_{\text{esc}} \approx 2.38 \text{ km/s}$$

### Step 4: Compare with Earth's Escape Velocity
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{2.38 \text{ km/s}}{11.2 \text{ km/s}} \approx 0.2125 \approx \frac{1}{4.7}$$

---

> **Result:** The escape velocity from the surface of the Moon is approximately **$2.38 \text{ km/s}$**, which is roughly **$\frac{1}{4.7}$** (or $21.3\%$) of Earth's escape velocity.
