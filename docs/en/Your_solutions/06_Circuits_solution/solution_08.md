## 8. AC Voltage Equation

### **Problem Statement**
The current in an AC circuit is given by the function:
$$I(t) = 2 \sin(120\pi t)$$
If the circuit consists of a single **$50\,\Omega$** resistor, what is the equation for the voltage $V(t)$ across it?

---



#### **STEP 1: Recall Ohm's Law for AC Circuits**
In a purely resistive AC circuit, the voltage and current are in phase. Ohm's Law applies to the instantaneous values just as it does to DC values.
* **Formula:** $V(t) = I(t) \times R$

#### **STEP 2: Identify Given Values**
* Current function: $I(t) = 2 \sin(120\pi t)$
* Resistance: $R = 50\,\Omega$

#### **STEP 3: Substitute and Multiply**
To find the voltage equation, multiply the entire current function by the resistance value.
* **Calculation:** $V(t) = [2 \sin(120\pi t)] \times 50$
* **Simplification:** $V(t) = (2 \times 50) \sin(120\pi t)$

#### **STEP 4: Formulate the Final Equation**
The peak voltage ($V_{peak}$) is calculated as $2\,\text{A} \times 50\,\Omega = 100\,\text{V}$. The frequency component ($120\pi$) remains unchanged.
* **Result:** $V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)$

---

### **Final Answer**
The equation for the voltage across the resistor is:
**$$V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)\,\text{V}$$**
