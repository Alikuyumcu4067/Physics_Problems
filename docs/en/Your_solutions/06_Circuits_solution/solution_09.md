## 9. Current as a Function of Time

### **Problem Statement**
The charge flowing through a wire is given by the function of time:
$$Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$$
What is the instantaneous current at **$t = 3\,\text{s}$**?

---



#### **STEP 1: Recall the Definition of Current**
Electric current ($I$) is defined as the rate of change of charge ($Q$) with respect to time ($t$). Mathematically, this is the first derivative of the charge function.
* **Formula:** $$I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}$$

#### **STEP 2: Take the Derivative of $Q(t)$**
Apply the power rule for differentiation ($d/dt [at^n] = n \cdot at^{n-1}$):
* **Function:** $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$
* **Derivative:** $\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2) + \frac{d}{dt}(5)$
* **Result:** $I(t) = 10t + 0 \Rightarrow \mathbf{I(t) = 10t}$

#### **STEP 3: Evaluate at the Given Time**
To find the specific current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$, substitute the value of $t$ into our derived current equation.
* **Substitution:** $I(3) = 10 \times 3$
* **Calculation:** $I(3) = 30$

---

### **Final Answer**
The instantaneous current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$ is:
**$$I = 30\,\text{A}$$**
