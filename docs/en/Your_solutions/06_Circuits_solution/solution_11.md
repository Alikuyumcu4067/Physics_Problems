## 11. Power & Energy

### **Problem Statement**
* What is the power ($P$) dissipated by a **$100\,\Omega$** resistor when a voltage of **50 V** is applied across it?
* How much energy ($E$) is consumed in **5 minutes**?

---



#### **STEP 1: Identify Given Data**
* Resistance ($R$) = $100\,\Omega$
* Voltage ($V$) = $50\,\text{V}$
* Time ($t$) = $5\,\text{minutes}$

#### **STEP 2: Calculate Power Dissipated**
Power can be calculated directly using voltage and resistance.
* **Formula:** $$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$$
* **Calculation:** $P = \frac{50^2}{100} = \frac{2500}{100}$
* **Result:** $P = \mathbf{25\,\text{W}}$ (Watts)

#### **STEP 3: Convert Time to Seconds**
Energy calculations in SI units require time in seconds to yield Joules.
* **Calculation:** $5\,\text{minutes} \times 60\,\text{seconds/minute} = \mathbf{300\,\text{s}}$

#### **STEP 4: Calculate Total Energy Consumed**
Energy is the product of power and time.
* **Formula:** $E = P \times t$
* **Calculation:** $E = 25\,\text{W} \times 300\,\text{s}$
* **Result:** $E = \mathbf{7,500\,\text{J}}$ (Joules)

---

### **Final Results**
| Parameter | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Power Dissipated** | $25\,\text{W}$ |
| **Energy Consumed (5 min)** | $7,500\,\text{J}$ (or $7.5\,\text{kJ}$) |
