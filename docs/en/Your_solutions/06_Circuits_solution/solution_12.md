## 12. Transformer Currents

### **Problem Statement**
A transformer has a primary coil with **1000 turns** and a secondary coil with **200 turns**.
* If the primary voltage is **120 V (AC)**, what is the secondary voltage ($V_s$)?
* If the current in the secondary is **3 A**, what is the current in the primary ($I_p$)?

---



#### **STEP 1: Identify Given Data**
* Primary Turns ($N_p$) = $1000$
* Secondary Turns ($N_s$) = $200$
* Primary Voltage ($V_p$) = $120\,\text{V}$
* Secondary Current ($I_s$) = $3\,\text{A}$

#### **STEP 2: Calculate Secondary Voltage ($V_s$)**
The ratio of voltages is equal to the ratio of the number of turns.
* **Formula:** $$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
* **Calculation:** $V_s = 120 \times \left( \frac{200}{1000} \right) = 120 \times 0.2$
* **Result:** $V_s = \mathbf{24\,\text{V}}$

#### **STEP 3: Calculate Primary Current ($I_p$)**
In an ideal transformer, power in equals power out ($V_p I_p = V_s I_s$). Therefore, the current ratio is the inverse of the turns ratio.
* **Formula:** $$\frac{I_p}{I_s} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
* **Calculation:** $I_p = 3 \times \left( \frac{200}{1000} \right) = 3 \times 0.2$
* **Result:** $I_p = \mathbf{0.6\,\text{A}}$

---

### **Final Results**
| Parameter | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secondary Voltage ($V_s$)** | $24\,\text{V}$ |
| **Primary Current ($I_p$)** | $0.6\,\text{A}$ |
