## 13. Transformer Ratio (Step-Down)

### **Problem Statement**
A transformer is used to step down the voltage from **120 V AC** to **9.0 V AC**. If the primary coil has **400 turns**, how many turns must the secondary coil ($N_s$) have?

---



#### **STEP 1: Identify Given Data**
* Primary Voltage ($V_p$) = $120\,\text{V}$
* Secondary Voltage ($V_s$) = $9.0\,\text{V}$
* Primary Turns ($N_p$) = $400\,\text{turns}$

#### **STEP 2: Use the Transformer Equation**
The relationship between the primary and secondary voltage and their respective number of turns is proportional.
* **Formula:** $$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

#### **STEP 3: Rearrange the Formula for $N_s$**
To find the number of secondary turns, rearrange the equation:
* **Rearranged Formula:** $$N_s = N_p \times \left( \frac{V_s}{V_p} \right)$$

#### **STEP 4: Perform the Calculation**
Substitute the known values into the equation:
* **Substitution:** $N_s = 400 \times \left( \frac{9.0}{120} \right)$
* **Calculation:** $N_s = 400 \times 0.075$
* **Result:** $N_s = \mathbf{30\,\text{turns}}$

---

### **Final Answer**
The secondary coil must have:
**$$N_s = 30\,\text{turns}$$**
