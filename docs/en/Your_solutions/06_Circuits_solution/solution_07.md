## 7. Capacitors in Parallel

### **Problem Statement**
Two capacitors, $C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a **10 V** battery.
* Calculate the total charge ($Q_{total}$) stored on the capacitors.
* Calculate the total energy ($U_{total}$) stored in the system.

---



#### **STEP 1: Calculate Total Capacitance ($C_{eq}$)**
For capacitors connected in parallel, the equivalent capacitance is the sum of individual capacitances.
* **Formula:** $C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$
* **Calculation:** $C_{eq} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} = \mathbf{10\,\mu\text{F}}$

#### **STEP 2: Calculate Total Charge ($Q_{total}$)**
The total charge stored is the product of the equivalent capacitance and the voltage.
* **Formula:** $Q = C_{eq} \cdot V$
* **Calculation:** $Q = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F} \times 10\,\text{V} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$
* **Result:** $Q = \mathbf{100\,\mu\text{C}}$ (or $10^{-4}\,\text{C}$)

#### **STEP 3: Calculate Total Energy Stored ($U_{total}$)**
The potential energy stored in a capacitor can be calculated using the voltage and capacitance.
* **Formula:** $U = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2$
* **Calculation:** $U = \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}) \times (10\,\text{V})^2$
* **Calculation:** $U = 0.5 \times 10^{-5} \times 100 = 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}$
* **Result:** $U = \mathbf{0.5\,\text{mJ}}$ (or $500\,\mu\text{J}$)

---

### **Final Summary**
| Parameter | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Equivalent Capacitance** | $10\,\mu\text{F}$ |
| **Total Charge Stored** | $100\,\mu\text{C}$ |
| **Total Energy Stored** | $0.5\,\text{mJ}$ |
