# Section 3: Waves

## 1. Wave Properties – Detailed Solution

### **Problem Statement**
A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$ (standard pitch A4). Given the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, determine its wavelength. Furthermore, calculate its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is significantly higher at $1482 \text{ m/s}$.

---

### **1.1. Parameters and Variables**
To solve this, we identify the physical constants provided:
* **Frequency ($f$):** $440 \text{ Hz}$ (or $440 \text{ s}^{-1}$)
* **Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$):** $1482 \text{ m/s}$
* **Unknowns:** Wavelength in air ($\lambda_{air}$) and Wavelength in water ($\lambda_{water}$)

---

### **1.2. Governing Physical Principle**
The fundamental relationship for any periodic wave relates its velocity ($v$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$). This is expressed by the **Wave Equation**:

$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To solve for the wavelength, we rearrange the formula:

$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

### **1.3. Step-by-Step Calculation**

#### **Step A: Wavelength in Air**
Substituting the values for the atmospheric medium:
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$
$$\lambda_{air} \approx \mathbf{0.7795 \text{ m}} \approx \mathbf{0.78 \text{ m}}$$

#### **Step B: Wavelength in Water**
Substituting the values for the aqueous medium:
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$
$$\lambda_{water} \approx \mathbf{3.3681 \text{ m}} \approx \mathbf{3.37 \text{ m}}$$

---

### **1.4. Comparative Results Table**

| Medium | Speed of Sound ($v$) | Frequency ($f$) | Wavelength ($\lambda$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Air** | $343 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | $\approx 0.78 \text{ m}$ |
| **Water** | $1482 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | $\approx 3.37 \text{ m}$ |

---

### **1.5. Physical Analysis and Discussion**
The most critical takeaway from this problem is the behavior of waves across different media:
1.  **Constancy of Frequency:** The frequency is determined solely by the source (the vibrating object). When the sound wave transitions from air to water, the frequency **does not change**.
2.  **Proportionality:** Because $v = f\lambda$, and $f$ is constant, the wavelength is **directly proportional** to the wave speed ($v \propto \lambda$). 
3.  **Conclusion:** Since water is a less compressible medium than air, sound travels roughly **4.3 times faster** in water. Consequently, the peaks of the sound wave are pushed further apart, resulting in a wavelength that is also **4.3 times longer** than in air.
