## Section 3: Waves

### 1. Wave Properties – Solution

**Problem:** A sound wave in air has a frequency of 440 Hz. If the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s, what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is 1482 m/s?

---

#### Given:
* **Frequency ($f$):** $440 \text{ Hz}$
* **Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$):** $1482 \text{ m/s}$

#### Formula:
The relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength is given by:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

#### Calculation:

**a) Wavelength in air ($\lambda_{air}$):**
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$
$$\lambda_{air} \approx \mathbf{0.78 \text{ m}}$$

**b) Wavelength in water ($\lambda_{water}$):**
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$
$$\lambda_{water} \approx \mathbf{3.37 \text{ m}}$$

---

> **Note:** When a wave travels from one medium to another (e.g., from air to water), its **frequency remains constant** because it is determined by the source. Since the speed of sound is much higher in water, the wavelength increases proportionally.
