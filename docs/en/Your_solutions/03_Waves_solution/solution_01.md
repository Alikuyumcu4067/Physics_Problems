# Section 3: Waves

## 1. Wave Properties – Formal Step-by-Step Solution

### **Problem Statement**
A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$. Given the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, calculate its wavelength. Furthermore, determine its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \text{ m/s}$.

---

### **Step 1: Identify and List Given Data**
To ensure accuracy, we first extract the known physical quantities from the problem:
* **Source Frequency ($f$):** $440 \text{ Hz}$ (Note: $1 \text{ Hz} = 1 \text{ s}^{-1}$)
* **Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$):** $1482 \text{ m/s}$

### **Step 2: State the Physical Principles and Assumptions**
* **Fundamental Principle:** The wavelength of a periodic wave is inversely proportional to its frequency and directly proportional to its speed in a given medium.
* **Key Assumption:** Frequency is a property of the source. When a wave moves from air to water, the **frequency remains constant ($f = 440 \text{ Hz}$)**, while the velocity and wavelength change.

### **Step 3: Select the Governing Equation**
We use the Universal Wave Equation:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To solve for the unknown wavelength ($\lambda$), we rearrange the equation as follows:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

### **Step 4: Detailed Calculation for Air ($\lambda_{air}$)**
Substitute the values for the air medium into the rearranged equation:
1.  **Formula:** $\lambda_{air} = \frac{v_{air}}{f}$
2.  **Substitution:** $\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ s}^{-1}}$
3.  **Calculation:** $\lambda_{air} \approx 0.779545... \text{ m}$
4.  **Result:** $\lambda_{air} \approx \mathbf{0.78 \text{ m}}$

### **Step 5: Detailed Calculation for Water ($\lambda_{water}$)**
Substitute the values for the water medium, maintaining the same frequency:
1.  **Formula:** $\lambda_{water} = \frac{v_{water}}{f}$
2.  **Substitution:** $\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ s}^{-1}}$
3.  **Calculation:** $\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368181... \text{ m}$
4.  **Result:** $\lambda_{water} \approx \mathbf{3.37 \text{ m}}$

---

### **Step 6: Summary and Physical Analysis**
| Medium | Velocity ($v$) | Frequency ($f$) | Wavelength ($\lambda$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Air** | $343 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | **$0.78 \text{ m}$** |
| **Water** | $1482 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | **$3.37 \text{ m}$** |

**Conclusion:** Since the speed of sound in water is approximately **4.3 times faster** than in air, the wavelength also increases by the same factor. This confirms the direct proportionality between $v$ and $\lambda$ when $f$ is constant.
