# Section 3: Waves

## 1. Wave Properties – Formal Solution

### **Problem Statement**
A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$. Given the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, calculate its wavelength. Furthermore, determine its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \text{ m/s}$.

---

### **1.1. Data Given**
* **Source Frequency ($f$):** $440 \text{ Hz}$
* **Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$):** $1482 \text{ m/s}$

### **1.2. Physical Assumptions**
1.  The medium is considered homogeneous.
2.  The frequency of a wave is determined by the source and remains **invariant** (constant) regardless of the medium change.

### **1.3. Fundamental Formula**
The relationship between the velocity of a wave ($v$), its frequency ($f$), and its wavelength ($\lambda$) is defined by:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To isolate the unknown variable ($\lambda$), we rearrange the equation:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

### **1.4. Detailed Calculation**

#### **Part A: Wavelength in Air ($\lambda_{air}$)**
Using the parameters for the air medium:
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ s}^{-1}}$$
$$\lambda_{air} \approx 0.779545... \text{ m}$$
**Rounding to two decimal places:**
$$\lambda_{air} \approx \mathbf{0.78 \text{ m}}$$

#### **Part B: Wavelength in Water ($\lambda_{water}$)**
Using the parameters for the water medium while keeping frequency constant:
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ s}^{-1}}$$
$$\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368181... \text{ m}$$
**Rounding to two decimal places:**
$$\lambda_{water} \approx \mathbf{3.37 \text{ m}}$$

---

### **1.5. Final Results Summary**
| Environment | Velocity ($v$) | Frequency ($f$) | Wavelength ($\lambda$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Air** | $343 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | **$0.78 \text{ m}$** |
| **Water** | $1482 \text{ m/s}$ | $440 \text{ Hz}$ | **$3.37 \text{ m}$** |

---

### **1.6. Conclusion and Physical Insight**
As the sound wave transitions from air to water, its speed increases by a factor of approximately $4.32$ ($1482 / 343$). Since the frequency is a constant property of the source, the wavelength must increase by the same factor to satisfy the wave equation. This explains why the wavelength in water is significantly longer than in air.
