## 6. Wave Equation

**Problem:**
A wave is described by the equation:
$$y(x, t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$$
where $x$ and $y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds. Determine the wave's:
a) Amplitude $A$.
b) Wavelength $\lambda$.
c) Frequency $f$.
d) Wave speed $v$.

**Solution:**
The standard form of a traveling wave equation is:
$$y(x, t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$

Where:
* $A$ = Amplitude
* $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$ (Wave number)
* $\omega = 2\pi f$ (Angular frequency)

**Step-by-Step Analysis:**

### 6.a) Amplitude ($A$)
By comparing the given equation with the standard form:
**Answer:** $A = 0.05 \text{ m}$

### 6.b) Wavelength ($\lambda$)
From the equation, $k = 2\pi$.
Using $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$:
$$2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = 1 \text{ m}$$
**Answer:** $\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$

### 6.c) Frequency ($f$)
From the equation, $\omega = 50\pi$.
Using $\omega = 2\pi f$:
$$50\pi = 2\pi f \implies f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ Hz}$$
**Answer:** $f = 25 \text{ Hz}$

### 6.d) Wave speed ($v$)
The wave speed can be calculated using $v = \lambda f$ or $v = \frac{\omega}{k}$:
$$v = 1 \text{ m} \times 25 \text{ Hz} = 25 \text{ m/s}$$
**Answer:** $v = 25 \text{ m/s}$
