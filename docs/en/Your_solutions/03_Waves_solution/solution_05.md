## 5. Echo Ranging

**Problem:**
A person shouts towards a cliff and hears the echo **1 second** later. How far away is the cliff? (Assume the speed of sound in air is **343 m/s**).

**Solution:**
When an echo is heard, the sound travels from the person to the cliff and then reflects back to the person. This means the total distance traveled by the sound is twice the distance to the cliff ($2d$).

The formula for distance is:
$$\text{Total Distance} = v \cdot t$$

Where:
* $v = 343 \text{ m/s}$ (Speed of sound)
* $t = 1 \text{ s}$ (Total time for the round trip)

**Step 1: Calculate the total distance traveled by the sound**
$$\text{Total Distance} = 343 \text{ m/s} \times 1 \text{ s} = 343 \text{ m}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the distance to the cliff ($d$)**
Since the sound traveled to the cliff and back, we divide the total distance by 2:
$$d = \frac{343 \text{ m}}{2}$$
$$d = 171.5 \text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The cliff is **171.5 meters** away.
