# 🎤 Magnetic Torque on a Current Loop 

## 🧾 Given

* Length: (l = 10 , \text{cm} = 0.10 , m)
* Width: (w = 5 , \text{cm} = 0.05 , m)
* Current: (I = 2 , A)
* Magnetic field: (B = 0.3 , T)

---

## 🎯 Objective

Find the **magnitude of the magnetic torque** acting on the loop.

---

## 📐 Step 1: Write the Formula

Using the torque relation from Magnetic Torque:

$$
\tau = I , A , B , \sin\theta
$$

### Explanation:

* Torque depends on current, loop area, magnetic field, and orientation.
* (\theta) is the angle between the **normal to the loop** and the magnetic field.

---

## 📌 Step 2: Determine the Angle

The magnetic field is **parallel to the plane of the loop**.

👉 This means:

* The **normal vector** is perpendicular to the plane
* So the angle between **normal** and **B** is:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

### Explanation:

This gives **maximum torque**.

---

## 📌 Step 3: Calculate the Area

$$
A = l \times w
$$

$$
A = 0.10 \times 0.05 = 0.005 , m^2
$$

### Explanation:

Area of a rectangle = length × width.

---

## 📌 Step 4: Substitute Values

$$
\tau = 2 \times 0.005 \times 0.3 \times 1
$$

### Explanation:

All known values are now inserted into the formula.

---

## 📌 Step 5: Perform the Calculation

$$
\tau = 2 \times 0.005 = 0.01
$$

$$
\tau = 0.01 \times 0.3 = 0.003
$$

---

## ✅ Final Result

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3} , \text{N·m}
$$

---

## 📌 Interpretation

* The torque tends to **rotate the loop**.
* Maximum torque occurs because the magnetic field is **parallel to the loop surface**.
* This principle is used in **electric motors**.
