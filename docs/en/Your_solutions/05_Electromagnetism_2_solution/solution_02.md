## 2. Ampere's Law: Superposition of Magnetic Fields

### 🔍 Problem Description
Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart, carrying $5 \text{ A}$ currents in **opposite directions**. We calculate the magnetic field magnitude and direction at the exact midpoint.

---

### ⚙️ The Solution Process

#### Step 1: Individual Field Magnitude
Using the formula for a long straight wire derived from **Ampere's Law**:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

Given:
- $I = 5 \text{ A}$
- $r = 0.05 \text{ m}$ (midpoint of $10 \text{ cm}$)
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

Calculation:
$$B_1 = B_2 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{0.05} = 2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$$

#### Step 2: Vector Direction Analysis
Applying the **Right-Hand Rule**:
1. For the first wire, the field at the midpoint points in a specific direction (e.g., into the page).
2. For the second wire (opposite current), the field at the midpoint points in the **same** direction.
3. Therefore, the total field is the arithmetic sum of the two.

#### Step 3: Final Result
$$B_{total} = B_1 + B_2 = 4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$$

---

### 💡 Presentation Note
**Crucial Observation:** If the currents were in the **same** direction, the magnetic fields at the midpoint would oppose each other, resulting in a net field of **zero**. Because they are in opposite directions, the fields reinforce each other at the center.
