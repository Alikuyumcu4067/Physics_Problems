# 🎤 Energy Stored in a Capacitor – Step-by-Step Solution

## 🧾 Given

* Plate area: (S = 0.02 , m^2)
* Distance between plates: (d = 5 , mm = 0.005 , m)
* Voltage: (U = 500 , V)

---

## 🎯 Objectives

We will calculate:

1. Capacitance (C)
2. Stored energy (U)
3. Electric field intensity (E)
4. Force between the plates (F)

---

## 📐 Step 1: Calculate Capacitance

Using the formula for a parallel-plate capacitor from Capacitance:

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

Where:

* (\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} , F/m)

---

### Substitution:

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot \frac{0.02}{0.005}
$$

---

### Calculation:

$$
\frac{0.02}{0.005} = 4
$$

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \times 4
$$

$$
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} , F
$$

---

## 📌 Step 2: Calculate Stored Energy

Using energy formula:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2
$$

---

### Substitution:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \cdot (500)^2
$$

---

### Calculation:

$$
(500)^2 = 250000
$$

$$
U = 0.5 \cdot 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 250000
$$

$$
U = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} , J
$$

---

## 📌 Step 3: Calculate Electric Field

Using:

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

---

### Substitution:

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005}
$$

---

### Calculation:

$$
E = 100000 = 1.0 \times 10^5 , V/m
$$

---

## 📌 Step 4: Calculate Force Between Plates

Using:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

---

### Substitution:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot (1.0 \times 10^5)^2 \cdot 0.02
$$

---

### Calculation:

$$
(1.0 \times 10^5)^2 = 1.0 \times 10^{10}
$$

$$
F = 0.5 \cdot 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 10^{10} \cdot 0.02
$$

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} , N
$$

---

## ✅ Final Results

* Capacitance:

$$
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} , F
$$

* Energy stored:

$$
U = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} , J
$$

* Electric field:

$$
E = 1.0 \times 10^5 , V/m
$$

* Force:

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} , N
$$
