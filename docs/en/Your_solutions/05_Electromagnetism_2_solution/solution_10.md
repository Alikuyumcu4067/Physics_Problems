#  Speed of Light in a Medium – 

## 🧾 Given

* Refractive index of diamond: (n = 2.42)
* Speed of light in vacuum: (c = 3.0 \times 10^8 , m/s)

---

## 🎯 Objective

Find the **speed of light in diamond**.

---

## 📐 Step 1: Use the Fundamental Relation

We start from the definition of refractive index from Refractive Index:

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

Where:

* (n) = refractive index
* (c) = speed of light in vacuum
* (v) = speed of light in the medium

---

## 📌 Step 2: Rearrange the Formula

We solve for (v):

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

Light slows down when it enters a material with higher refractive index.

---

## 📌 Step 3: Substitute the Values

$$
v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

We insert the known values for vacuum speed and diamond’s refractive index.

---

## 📌 Step 4: Perform the Calculation

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 , m/s
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

Dividing by 2.42 reduces the speed significantly because diamond is optically dense.

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 , m/s}
$$

---

## 📌 Physical Interpretation

* Light travels slower in diamond compared to air or vacuum.
* A higher refractive index means **stronger slowing effect**.
* This is why diamond has strong optical effects like brilliance and dispersion.
