# 🔍 Physics Lab: Biot-Savart Law Analysis

## 3. Magnetic Field Contribution of a Short Current Segment

### 📝 Executive Summary
This calculation determines the magnetic field generated by a discrete wire segment. This is a fundamental application of the **Biot-Savart Law**, demonstrating how localized currents influence space at a distance.

---

### ⚙️ Mathematical Derivation

#### 1. The Governing Equation
For a small segment where the length is significantly less than the distance ($\Delta L \ll r$), we use:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I \Delta L \sin \theta}{r^2}$$

#### 2. Variables & Constants
* **Current ($I$):** $3\text{ A}$
* **Length ($\Delta L$):** $0.1\text{ m}$
* **Distance ($r$):** $0.2\text{ m}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (Maximum field intensity)
* **$\mu_0/4\pi$:** $10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

#### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation
- **Numerator:** $3 \text{ A} \times 0.1 \text{ m} = 0.3 \text{ A}\cdot\text{m}$
- **Denominator:** $(0.2 \text{ m})^2 = 0.04 \text{ m}^2$
- **Result:** $B = 10^{-7} \times \frac{0.3}{0.04} = 7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$

---

### 💡 Presentation Deep-Dive (Key Insights)

* **Directionality:** Based on the **Right-Hand Rule**, if the current flows along the x-axis and point $P$ is on the y-axis, the magnetic field vector points strictly along the **z-axis**.
* **The Inverse Square Law:** Notice that the magnetic field strength drops off with the **square** of the distance ($r^2$). Doubling the distance to $0.4\text{ m}$ would reduce the field by a factor of 4.
* **Vector Cross Product:** The use of $\sin(90^\circ)$ represents the cross product magnitude between the current direction and the radial vector.

> [!TIP]
> **Sunum İpucu:** Dinleyicilere bu formülün neden $2\pi r$ değil de $r^2$ olduğunu açıklarken; $2\pi r$'nin **sonsuz uzunluktaki** teller için (tüm segmentlerin toplamı), $r^2$ içeren bu formülün ise sadece **küçük bir parça** için olduğunu belirtmek profesyonelliğini gösterir.
