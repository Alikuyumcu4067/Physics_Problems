# Solution 9: Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement
Which object has greater momentum?
1. **A Fly:** Mass ($m_1$) = 2 grams, Velocity ($v_1$) = 10 m/s
2. **A Tennis Ball:** Mass ($m_2$) = 60 grams, Velocity ($v_2$) = 1 m/s

---

## 1. Formula
Momentum ($p$) is the product of an object's mass and its velocity:
$$p = m \cdot v$$

*Note: To compare them correctly, we must convert mass from grams to kilograms ($1 \text{ kg} = 1000 \text{ g}$).*

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Momentum of the Fly ($p_1$)
- $m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
- $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$
$$p_1 = 0.002 \cdot 10 = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

### Step 2: Momentum of the Tennis Ball ($p_2$)
- $m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$
- $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$
$$p_2 = 0.060 \cdot 1 = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

---

## 3. Conclusion
Comparing the results:
- **Fly:** $0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
- **Tennis Ball:** $0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$

Since $0.06 > 0.02$, the **tennis ball** has greater momentum, even though it is moving 10 times slower than the fly. This is because its mass is 30 times larger.
