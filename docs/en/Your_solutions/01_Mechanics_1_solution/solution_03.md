# Problem 3: Uniform Circular Motion

**Problem Statement:**
A particle moves in a circle of radius $r = 2.0 \text{ m}$ with a constant speed $v = 4.0 \text{ m/s}$. Find the period of motion and the magnitude of the centripetal acceleration.

---

### a) Period of Motion ($T$)
The period is the time required for one complete revolution. It is calculated using the circumference of the circle:
$$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v}$$
Substituting the given values ($r = 2.0, v = 4.0$):
$$T = \frac{2\pi (2.0)}{4.0} = \frac{4\pi}{4.0} = \pi \approx \mathbf{3.14 \text{ s}}$$

### b) Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)
The acceleration directed toward the center of the circle is given by:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
Substituting the given values:
$$a_c = \frac{4.0^2}{2.0} = \frac{16}{2.0} = \mathbf{8.0 \text{ m/s}^2}$$

### c) Velocity Vector ($\vec{v}$)
Note that while the speed is constant, the velocity vector is constantly changing direction, which is why there is a non-zero acceleration.
$$\vec{a}_c \perp \vec{v}$$
