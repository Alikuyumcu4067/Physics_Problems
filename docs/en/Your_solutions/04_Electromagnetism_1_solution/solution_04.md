# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Problem 4

## 4. Force Comparison: Electrostatic vs. Gravitational Forces

**Problem:** Calculate the magnitude of the electric force ($F_e$) and the gravitational force ($F_g$) between an electron and a proton in a hydrogen atom (average distance $r \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$). What is the ratio $F_e/F_g$?

---

### I. Fundamental Constants and Data
To perform this calculation, we use the following standard physical constants:
* **Proton charge ($e$):** $+1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Electron charge ($-e$):** $-1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Electron mass ($m_e$):** $9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Proton mass ($m_p$):** $1.673 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb's constant ($k$):** $8.987 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Universal Gravitational constant ($G$):** $6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$

### II. Calculation of Electric Force ($F_e$)
According to **Coulomb's Law**:
$$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
$$F_e = \frac{(8.987 \times 10^9) (1.602 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e \approx 8.22 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$

### III. Calculation of Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
According to **Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation**:
$$F_g = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
$$F_g = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) (9.109 \times 10^{-31}) (1.673 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g \approx 3.63 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$

### IV. Calculation of the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
The ratio describes the relative strength of these two fundamental forces:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.22 \times 10^{-8}}{3.63 \times 10^{-47}}$$
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.26 \times 10^{39}$$

---

### Final Result
* **Electric Force:** $F_e \approx 8.22 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$
* **Gravitational Force:** $F_g \approx 3.63 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$
* **Ratio ($F_e / F_g$):** $\approx 2.26 \times 10^{39}$

**Key Insight:** The electric force is approximately **39 orders of magnitude** stronger than the gravitational force. This confirms that in atomic and molecular physics, gravity is so weak that it can be entirely ignored when considering the interactions between subatomic particles.
