# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Problem 10

## 10. Lorentz Force Acting on a Wire: Angular Dependence

**Problem:** A straight wire $2.0 \text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10 \text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5 \text{ T}$. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:
a) $90^\circ$
b) $45^\circ$
c) $0^\circ$

---

### I. Physical Principles
The magnetic force exerted on a straight segment of wire carrying a steady current in a uniform magnetic field is given by the formula:
$$\vec{F} = I (\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$$

The magnitude of this force is calculated as:
$$F = I L B \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* $I$ is the current in Amperes ($A$).
* $L$ is the length of the wire in meters ($m$).
* $B$ is the magnetic field strength in Tesla ($T$).
* $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current and the direction of the magnetic field.

### II. Given Data
* **Current ($I$):** $10 \text{ A}$
* **Length ($L$):** $2.0 \text{ m}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5 \text{ T}$

### III. Calculations for Different Angles

#### a) Angle $\theta = 90^\circ$
When the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. This results in the maximum possible force:
$$F = (10 \text{ A}) \cdot (2.0 \text{ m}) \cdot (0.5 \text{ T}) \cdot \sin(90^\circ)$$
$$F = 10 \text{ N}$$

#### b) Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$
When the wire is at a $45^\circ$ angle, $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$:
$$F = (10 \text{ A}) \cdot (2.0 \text{ m}) \cdot (0.5 \text{ T}) \cdot \sin(45^\circ)$$
$$F = 10 \cdot 0.707$$
$$F \approx 7.07 \text{ N}$$

#### c) Angle $\theta = 0^\circ$
When the wire is parallel to the magnetic field, $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$:
$$F = (10 \text{ A}) \cdot (2.0 \text{ m}) \cdot (0.5 \text{ T}) \cdot \sin(0^\circ)$$
$$F = 0 \text{ N}$$

---

### Final Results Summary
| Angle ($\theta$) | Force Magnitude ($F$) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **90° (Perpendicular)** | **10.0 N** |
| **45° (Diagonal)** | **7.07 N** |
| **0° (Parallel)** | **0.0 N** |

**Key Insight:** The magnetic force is highly dependent on the orientation of the conductor. No force is exerted if the current flows along the magnetic field lines, whereas the force is maximized when the current cuts across the field lines perpendicularly.
