# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Problem 7

## 7. Cyclotron Motion: Particle Trajectory in a Magnetic Field

**Problem:** An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $5000 \text{ V}$. It then enters a region of uniform magnetic field $B = 0.1 \text{ T}$, perpendicular to its velocity. What is the radius of the circular path it will follow?

---

### I. Physical Constants (Electron)
* **Mass ($m_e$):** $\approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Charge ($e$):** $\approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$

### II. Step 1: Calculating Velocity ($v$)
When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference ($V$), its electrical potential energy is converted into kinetic energy:
$$eV = \frac{1}{2} m_e v^2$$

Solving for velocity ($v$):
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \cdot (5000 \text{ V})}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}}}$$
$$v \approx \sqrt{1.756 \times 10^{15}} \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$$

### III. Step 2: Calculating the Radius ($r$)
When a charged particle enters a magnetic field perpendicularly, the **Lorentz force** acts as a centripetal force, causing circular motion:
$$F_B = F_c \implies evB = \frac{m_e v^2}{r}$$

Rearranging to find the radius ($r$):
$$r = \frac{m_e v}{eB}$$

Substituting the values:
$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \cdot (4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s})}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \cdot (0.1 \text{ T})}$$
$$r = \frac{3.817 \times 10^{-23}}{1.6 \times 10^{-20}}$$
$$r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Result
The radius of the circular path is:
**$r \approx 2.38 \text{ mm}$**

**Key Insight:** The radius of the path is proportional to the momentum ($mv$) of the particle and inversely proportional to the strength of the magnetic field. This principle is widely used in mass spectrometers and particle accelerators (cyclotrons).
