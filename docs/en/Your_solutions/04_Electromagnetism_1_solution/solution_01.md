#  Coulomb’s Law – Electric Force at Center 

## 🧾 Given

* Four point charges: (+1.0 , C) each
* Arranged at the corners of a square
* Side length of square: (a = 1.0 , m)
* Test charge at center: (q = -2.0 , C)

---

## 🎯 Objective

Find the **magnitude and direction of the net electric force** on the charge at the center.

---

## 📐 Step 1: Use Coulomb’s Law

We start from the fundamental law from Coulomb's Law:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

This law describes the force between two point charges depending on:

* charge magnitude
* distance between them
* Coulomb constant

---

## 📌 Step 2: Determine Geometry

* The charge is at the **center of a square**
* All four corner charges are **equidistant from the center**

### Distance from center to each corner:

$$
r = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1.0}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707 , m
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

This comes from the diagonal of a square.

---

## 📌 Step 3: Force from One Corner Charge

$$
F_1 = k \frac{|(1.0)(2.0)|}{(0.707)^2}
$$

$$
F_1 = 9 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{2}{0.5}
$$

$$
F_1 = 9 \times 10^9 \cdot 4
$$

$$
F_1 = 3.6 \times 10^{10} , N
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

Each corner exerts an attractive force toward itself (since center charge is negative).

---

## 📌 Step 4: Analyze Symmetry

### 💡 Key Insight:

* Forces from opposite corners are equal in magnitude
* But act in **opposite directions**
* Therefore they cancel pairwise

---

## 📌 Step 5: Net Force Result

* Top-left cancels bottom-right
* Top-right cancels bottom-left

$$
F_{net} = 0
$$

### 💡 Explanation:

Perfect symmetry leads to complete cancellation of all vector forces.

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{F = 0 , N}
$$

---

## 📌 Physical Interpretation

* Even though each charge exerts a strong force individually,
* The symmetric arrangement causes **complete cancellation**
* This is a classic example of **vector symmetry in electrostatics**
