# Section 4: Electromagnetism I - Problem 5

## 5. Field Levitation: Balancing Electrostatic and Gravitational Forces

**Problem:** What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? 

**Given Data:**
* Mass of proton ($m_p$): $\approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* Charge of proton ($e$): $\approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* Gravitational acceleration ($g$): $\approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### I. Physical Principles
For an object to **levitate** (remain stationary in mid-air), the net force acting on it must be zero ($\sum F = 0$). In this scenario, two primary forces are acting on the proton:
1.  **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Acts vertically downward, pulling the proton toward the Earth.
2.  **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Must act vertically upward to counteract gravity.

### II. Equilibrium Equation
We set the magnitude of the upward electric force equal to the magnitude of the downward gravitational force:
$$F_e = F_g$$
$$q E = m_p g$$

Where:
* $q$ is the charge of the proton ($e$)
* $E$ is the electric field strength

### III. Calculation
To find the required electric field strength ($E$), we rearrange the formula:
$$E = \frac{m_p g}{e}$$

Substituting the given values:
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$
$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.022875 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$$

### IV. Direction of the Field
Since a proton is a **positively charged** particle, the electric force it experiences is in the same direction as the electric field. To create an upward force, the electric field must be directed **vertically upward**.

---

### Final Result
* **Electric Field Strength:** $E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$
* **Direction:** Vertically Upward
