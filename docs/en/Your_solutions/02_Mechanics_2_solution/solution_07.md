## 7. Dynamics with Friction (Stacked Blocks)

### Problem Statement
A **5 kg** block (Block 1) is placed on a **10 kg** block (Block 2). A horizontal force of **45 N** is applied to the 10 kg block. The 5 kg block is tied to a wall with a string, preventing it from moving. The coefficient of kinetic friction ($\mu_k$) between all moving surfaces is **0.2**. Find the acceleration of the 10 kg block.
*(Assume $g = 9.81 \, m/s^2$)*

---

### Analytical Solution

To find the acceleration of the 10 kg block, we must identify all horizontal forces acting on it.

#### Step 1: Calculate Friction Forces
There are two surfaces where friction occurs for the 10 kg block:
1. **Top Surface ($f_1$):** Friction between the 5 kg block and the 10 kg block.
   - Normal force ($N_1$) = $m_1 g = 5 \times 9.81 = 49.05 \, N$
   - $f_1 = \mu_k \cdot N_1 = 0.2 \times 49.05 = \mathbf{9.81 \, N}$
2. **Bottom Surface ($f_2$):** Friction between the 10 kg block and the floor.
   - Normal force ($N_2$) = $(m_1 + m_2)g = (5 + 10) \times 9.81 = 147.15 \, N$
   - $f_2 = \mu_k \cdot N_2 = 0.2 \times 147.15 = \mathbf{29.43 \, N}$
