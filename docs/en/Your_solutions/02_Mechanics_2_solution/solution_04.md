## 4. Energy & Momentum (Colliding Blocks)

### Problem Statement
A **0.5 kg** block slides down a frictionless track from a height of **3.0 m**. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a **1.5 kg** block, which is initially at rest. What is the speed of the combined mass just after the collision?
*(Assume $g = 9.81 \, m/s^2$)*

---

### Analytical Solution

This problem is solved in two distinct stages: **Conservation of Energy** (to find the speed before impact) and **Conservation of Momentum** (to find the speed after impact).

#### Step 1: Conservation of Energy (The Slide)
The gravitational potential energy at the top is converted into kinetic energy at the bottom.
$$m_1 gh = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2$$

The mass $m_1$ cancels out. Solving for $v_1$ (the velocity of the first block at the bottom):
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \times 9.81 \times 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.86} \approx \mathbf{7.67 \, m/s}$$

#### Step 2: Conservation of Momentum (The Collision)
Since the blocks stick together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**. Total momentum before the collision equals total momentum after the collision.
$$m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) V_{final}$$

Given:
- $m_1 = 0.5 \, kg$, $v_1 = 7.67 \, m/s$
- $m_2 = 1.5 \, kg$, $v_2 = 0$ (at rest)

$$0.5 \times 7.67 + 0 = (0.5 + 1.5) \cdot V_{final}$$
$$3.835 = 2.0 \cdot V_{final}$$

#### Step 3: Final Calculation
$$V_{final} = \frac{3.835}{2.0} \approx \mathbf{1.92 \, m/s}$$

**Result:** The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately **1.92 m/s**.
